# Brag Plan: Kisan Alert

## What is this app?
Kisan Alert is a voice-first farm advisory app concept for Indian farmers that keeps the experience focused on one clear next step at a time.

## The angle
This version should feel less like a social clip and more like a patient product walkthrough. The point is not speed. The point is trust, readability, and showing that the product is built for real farmer constraints: low-text interaction, local language use, shared phones, short action-oriented guidance, and clear escalation paths.

## Hook (first 6-8 seconds)
Open with the core claim, let it breathe, and make the product intent obvious before any UI details appear:
`Built for low-text, local-language farming use`
`One clear farming action at a time.`

## Key moments (the middle)
- Show who the product is for before showing any feature.
- Let the assisted sign-in and shared-phone context read clearly.
- Hold on Home long enough to understand the action, weather signal, and trust framing.
- Separate the Ask question from the Ask answer instead of combining them into one fast scene.
- Let Alerts and Account land as trust and continuity features, not just extra tabs.

## Outro / punchline
End on the full active flow and the product promise:
`Home. Ask. Alerts. Account.`
`Short guidance that farmers can actually use in the field.`

## User flow worth showing
Farmer context -> assisted sign-in -> Home daily action -> Ask question -> short answer with steps -> Alerts -> Account and support framing.

## Tone
- Preset: polished
- Creative direction: calm, trustworthy hackathon demo film for a real farmer-facing product
- Interpretation: slower pacing, longer holds, fewer simultaneous elements, and stronger emphasis on reading comfort.

## Format: landscape - 1920x1080
## Duration: 88 seconds

## Share copy (draft)
Kisan Alert is a slower, clearer farming guidance experience: local-language support, one short answer at a time, and daily actions that make sense on a shared phone in the field.

## Audio direction
- Role: warm bed
- Music: `happy-beats-business-moves-vol-11-by-ende-dot-app.mp3`
- Music treatment: low volume throughout, with long readable pauses and a softer close
- Music cue guidance: no beat-driven pacing; readability takes priority over rhythmic edits
- Audio-reactive treatment: none
- SFX posture: none in this cut unless needed later
- Audio-coupled moments: only gentle scene changes
- Restraint rule: nothing in the soundtrack should make the product feel rushed, flashy, or gimmicky.
