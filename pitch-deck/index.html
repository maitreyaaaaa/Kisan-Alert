Slide 2
Problem

Farm advice is available, but not always accessible when it matters.

Farmers need timely, local, understandable guidance. Existing access is often fragmented, text-heavy, or dependent on limited human reach.

1:650 India's extension agent-to-farmer ratio cited in the Digital Green case study.
Low-literacy barrier Voice and simpler interfaces matter for many new internet users.
Shared-device reality Rural usage often happens on family phones, not personal always-on devices.

Research basis includes Google on voice-first internet use and the Digital Green case study on extension capacity.

Slide 3
Why Existing UX Breaks

Generic chat interfaces do not fit real farmer usage.

  • Google states voice is critical for many new internet users with low literacy.
  • Typing in scripted languages can add friction where speaking is easier.
  • Long answers are harder to trust and act on in the field.
  • CGAP notes women often face lower traditional or digital literacy and lower smartphone/mobile internet access.
  • Voice, pictures, and local-language interaction reduce digital-literacy barriers.
  • Trust and usability matter as much as raw model capability.
Slide 4
Solution

Kisan Alert reduces the product to four farmer-safe surfaces.

Instead of a dense agriculture super-app, the product focuses on a few tasks it can do clearly.

Home
Ask
Alerts
Account
  • Home: district-aware daily actions
  • Ask: voice, photo, or typed query
  • Alerts: short local warnings and next steps
  • Account: language, crop, district, voice, helper
Slide 5
Who It Serves

Designed for farmers in tier 2 and tier 3 India.

  • Partial or inconsistent literacy is possible.
  • Screen use may happen outdoors in daylight.
  • The device may be shared with family members.
  • Short, local-language guidance builds confidence faster than long text.

Design Consequences

  • Voice-first interaction
  • One answer card, not raw model output
  • Visible helper and escalation path
  • Simple settings, high-contrast visuals
Slide 6
How The AI Works

AI is used as a constrained advisory layer, not a free-form chatbot.

Voice / Image / Text
Query Interpretation
Retrieve Approved Context
Generate Structured Output
Render Short Answer Card
Human Review If Needed

Output Contract

  • summary
  • actions
  • risk
  • confidence
  • needsHumanReview

Current Prototype Path

  • Android prototype currently uses Gemini for advisory flows.
  • Production target moves model access server-side.
  • The client should only render farmer-safe structured outputs.
Slide 7
Safety And Trust

This is not an unconstrained AI chatbot.

  • Constrained JSON answer schema
  • Short summary plus 1 to 3 next steps
  • Human-review framing for lower-confidence advice
  • Planned audit and safety logging service
  • Digital Green's production pattern uses validated knowledge sources.
  • Their early rollout added a human review stage by assisting extension agents instead of replacing them.
  • Kisan Alert follows the same trust direction: assist, constrain, escalate.
Slide 8
